# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Applies To:** All full-time and part-time employees of Meridian Technologies, Inc.

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for the accrual, usage, and management of Paid Time Off (PTO) at Meridian Technologies. PTO provides employees with flexible time away from work for rest, personal activities, illness, and other needs.

## 2. Eligibility

All regular full-time and part-time employees are eligible for PTO benefits beginning on their first day of employment. Temporary employees, contractors, and interns are not eligible for PTO.

## 3. PTO Accrual Rates

### 3.1 Full-Time Employees

PTO accrues based on length of service as follows:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Days | Monthly Accrual |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| 0–2 years       | 15 days         | 1.25 days       |
| 3–5 years       | 20 days         | 1.67 days       |
| 6–10 years      | 25 days         | 2.08 days       |
| 11+ years       | 30 days         | 2.50 days       |

### 3.2 Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees accrue PTO on a prorated basis relative to their scheduled hours. For example, an employee working 20 hours per week accrues at 50% of the full-time rate.

### 3.3 Accrual Cap

PTO accrual is capped at 1.5 times the employee's annual entitlement. Once the cap is reached, no additional PTO accrues until the balance falls below the cap. For example, an employee entitled to 20 days per year stops accruing upon reaching 30 days.

## 4. Using PTO

### 4.1 Request Process

Employees must submit PTO requests through the HR portal at least:
- **5 business days** in advance for 1–3 days of PTO
- **10 business days** in advance for 4–5 days of PTO
- **30 days** in advance for requests exceeding 5 consecutive days

Managers must approve or deny requests within 2 business days of submission.

### 4.2 Minimum Increments

PTO may be taken in increments of no less than half a day (4 hours). There is no minimum increment for sick-related PTO usage.

### 4.3 Blackout Periods

The company may designate blackout periods during peak business seasons when PTO requests may be limited or denied. Blackout periods will be announced at least 60 days in advance and will not exceed 4 weeks per calendar year.

Current standing blackout periods:
- Last two weeks of December (December 15–31)
- First week of annual product launch (typically mid-March)

### 4.4 Consecutive Days

Employees may take up to 10 consecutive business days of PTO without VP-level approval. Requests for more than 10 consecutive days require written approval from the employee's Vice President and HR.

## 5. PTO Carryover

### 5.1 Annual Carryover

Employees may carry over up to 5 unused PTO days into the following calendar year. Carryover days must be used by March 31 of the new year or they will be forfeited.

### 5.2 Exceptions

In exceptional circumstances (e.g., company-imposed blackout prevented usage), an employee may request additional carryover through a written petition to HR. Approval is at the discretion of the VP of Human Resources.

## 6. PTO and Termination

### 6.1 Voluntary Resignation

Employees who voluntarily resign will be paid out for accrued, unused PTO up to a maximum of 10 days in their final paycheck, provided they give at least 2 weeks' notice.

### 6.2 Involuntary Termination

Employees who are involuntarily terminated will receive payout for accrued, unused PTO up to a maximum of 5 days, unless termination is for cause involving gross misconduct.

## 7. Unplanned Absences

Employees who are unable to work due to sudden illness or emergency must notify their manager within 1 hour of their scheduled start time. Three or more consecutive unplanned absence days require a doctor's note upon return.

## 8. PTO Donation

Employees with more than 10 accrued PTO days may donate up to 5 days per year to a colleague facing a medical emergency or extended family crisis, subject to HR approval.
