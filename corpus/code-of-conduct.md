# Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department & Legal Department
**Applies To:** All employees, contractors, interns, and board members of Meridian Technologies, Inc.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards and behavioral expectations for everyone associated with Meridian Technologies. We are committed to fostering a workplace culture built on integrity, respect, and professionalism.

## 2. Core Values

Meridian Technologies operates according to these core values:
- **Integrity**: We act honestly and transparently in all our dealings
- **Respect**: We treat every individual with dignity and fairness
- **Excellence**: We strive for the highest quality in our work
- **Collaboration**: We work together and value diverse perspectives
- **Accountability**: We take ownership of our actions and their impact

## 3. Professional Behavior

### 3.1 Workplace Conduct

All personnel are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with courtesy and professionalism
- Communicate respectfully in all channels (in-person, email, chat, video)
- Be punctual for meetings and commitments
- Dress appropriately for the work setting (business casual for office, professional for client meetings)
- Maintain a clean and organized workspace
- Avoid disruptive behavior that impedes others' ability to work

### 3.2 Confidentiality

Employees must protect confidential business information and not disclose it to unauthorized parties. This obligation continues after employment ends. Confidential information includes but is not limited to:
- Product roadmaps and technical specifications
- Customer data and contracts
- Financial information and projections
- Employee personal information
- Trade secrets and proprietary algorithms

## 4. Anti-Harassment and Non-Discrimination

### 4.1 Zero Tolerance

Meridian Technologies has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, and bullying. This applies to all forms including but not limited to:
- Sexual harassment (unwelcome advances, requests for favors, verbal or physical conduct)
- Racial, ethnic, or national origin harassment
- Religious harassment
- Age-based harassment
- Disability-based harassment
- Gender identity or sexual orientation harassment
- Cyber-bullying or harassment through digital communications

### 4.2 Protected Characteristics

Employment decisions and workplace treatment must not be based on:
race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, marital status, pregnancy, or any other legally protected characteristic.

### 4.3 Reporting Harassment

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it through any of the following channels:
1. Direct manager (unless the manager is the subject of the complaint)
2. HR department: hr@meridiantech.com or extension 4200
3. Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-ETHIC (1-800-555-38442)
4. Chief People Officer: directly via email or in person

All reports will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and confidentially. Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment in good faith is strictly prohibited and will result in severe disciplinary action.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### 5.1 Definition

A conflict of interest arises when personal interests could compromise or appear to compromise professional judgment. Examples include:
- Having a financial interest in a competitor, supplier, or customer
- Hiring or supervising a family member or close personal friend
- Accepting gifts or entertainment that could influence business decisions
- Using company resources for personal benefit
- Taking a second job that conflicts with company duties

### 5.2 Disclosure

Employees must promptly disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their manager and the Legal department. Disclosure does not necessarily disqualify the employee but allows proper management of the situation.

### 5.3 Gifts and Entertainment

- Employees may accept modest gifts valued at **$50 or less** per occasion
- Gifts exceeding $50 must be reported to the Legal department
- Cash gifts or gift cards are never acceptable
- Entertainment (meals, events) with clients or vendors must have a legitimate business purpose

## 6. Substance Use

### 6.1 Alcohol

Moderate alcohol consumption is permitted at company-sponsored events. Employees are expected to drink responsibly and must not become intoxicated at work events. Alcohol is never permitted in the workplace during business hours.

### 6.2 Drugs

The use, possession, distribution, or sale of illegal substances on company premises or during work hours is strictly prohibited. Employees must not report to work under the influence of any substance that impairs their ability to perform their duties safely and effectively.

### 6.3 Prescription Medications

Employees taking prescription medications that may affect their ability to work safely should notify HR (disclosure of the specific medication is not required — only the potential impact on work duties).

## 7. Social Media

### 7.1 Personal Social Media

Employees are free to use personal social media accounts but must:
- Clearly state that views expressed are their own and not those of Meridian Technologies
- Not disclose confidential company information
- Not make defamatory statements about the company, colleagues, or clients
- Not use company logos or branding without Marketing approval

### 7.2 Official Company Accounts

Only authorized Marketing team members may post on behalf of Meridian Technologies on social media platforms.

## 8. Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct will be addressed through the company's disciplinary process:
1. **Verbal warning** for minor first-time offenses
2. **Written warning** for repeat minor offenses or moderate violations
3. **Suspension** for serious violations pending investigation
4. **Termination** for gross misconduct, repeated violations, or criminal behavior

The severity of disciplinary action will be proportional to the nature of the violation, considering context, intent, and impact.

## 9. Reporting Violations

Individuals who become aware of Code violations should report them through the channels listed in Section 4.3. All established employees are obligated to report suspected violations. Good-faith reporters are protected from retaliation.

## 10. Acknowledgment

All employees must acknowledge receipt and understanding of this Code of Conduct upon hire and annually thereafter through the HR portal.

## 11. Contact

For questions about the Code of Conduct, contact the Legal department at legal@meridiantech.com or extension 5000, or HR at hr@meridiantech.com or extension 4200.
