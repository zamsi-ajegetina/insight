# Remote Work Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department
**Applies To:** All eligible employees of Meridian Technologies, Inc.

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the guidelines and expectations for employees who work remotely, whether on a full-time, part-time, or occasional basis. Meridian Technologies supports flexible work arrangements that maintain productivity while offering employees a better work-life balance.

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Eligible Roles

Remote work eligibility is determined by the nature of the role and the employee's performance. The following criteria must be met:
- The role's responsibilities can be effectively performed remotely
- The employee has completed their probationary period (first 90 days)
- The employee has a satisfactory or above performance rating
- The employee has reliable internet access (minimum 50 Mbps download)
- Manager and VP approval have been obtained

### 2.2 Ineligible Roles

Roles requiring physical presence (e.g., facilities management, lab technicians, front desk reception) are not eligible for remote work.

## 3. Remote Work Arrangements

### 3.1 Hybrid Schedule

The default arrangement is a hybrid model: employees work **3 days in-office and 2 days remote** per week. The specific in-office days are determined by department:
- **Engineering**: Monday, Tuesday, Thursday in-office
- **Marketing & Sales**: Monday, Wednesday, Thursday in-office
- **Operations & Finance**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday in-office
- **HR & Legal**: Monday, Wednesday, Friday in-office

### 3.2 Fully Remote

Fully remote arrangements are available for employees who:
- Live more than 75 miles from the nearest office
- Have been with the company for 1+ years with consistently strong performance
- Have VP-level approval

Fully remote employees must visit the office at least once per quarter for team events and planning sessions.

### 3.3 Occasional Remote Work

All eligible employees may work remotely on an ad-hoc basis (e.g., waiting for a delivery, minor illness) up to 3 additional days per month beyond their hybrid schedule, with same-day manager notification.

## 4. Workspace Requirements

### 4.1 Home Office Setup

Remote employees must maintain a dedicated workspace that:
- Is quiet and free from distractions during work hours
- Has a stable internet connection (minimum 50 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload)
- Has adequate lighting for video calls
- Is ergonomically appropriate (desk and chair at proper height)

### 4.2 Equipment

Meridian Technologies provides the following for remote work:
- Laptop computer (standard company-issued)
- External monitor (one 27" monitor upon request)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset for video calls

Employees may request a one-time **$500 home office stipend** for ergonomic furniture (desk, chair) after completing 6 months of employment. Approval is through HR.

## 5. Availability and Communication

### 5.1 Core Hours

All remote employees must be available during **core hours: 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM** in their local time zone. Outside core hours, employees may flex their remaining work time to complete their required hours.

### 5.2 Response Times

- **Slack messages**: Respond within 30 minutes during core hours
- **Emails**: Respond within 4 hours during business hours
- **Calendar invitations**: Accept or decline within 24 hours

### 5.3 Video Calls

Cameras should be turned on for team meetings, 1:1s, and client calls unless bandwidth constraints prevent it. Virtual backgrounds are acceptable.

### 5.4 Status Updates

Remote employees must keep their Slack status updated to reflect their current availability (e.g., "In a meeting," "Lunch break," "Focused work — slow responses").

## 6. Performance Management

### 6.1 Expectations

Remote employees are held to the same performance standards and deliverables as in-office employees. Managers will evaluate remote workers based on output and results, not hours logged.

### 6.2 Regular Check-ins

Managers should conduct weekly 1:1 meetings with remote team members to discuss progress, blockers, and well-being.

### 6.3 Revocation

Remote work privileges may be revoked if:
- Performance declines below satisfactory levels
- The employee is consistently unavailable during core hours
- The arrangement no longer meets business needs
- The employee fails to maintain required workspace standards

Revocation requires a 30-day transition period during which the employee must resume in-office work.

## 7. Time Tracking

Remote employees in non-exempt (hourly) positions must log their work hours daily using the company time-tracking system. Exempt (salaried) employees are expected to work their standard schedule but are not required to track hours unless requested by their manager.

## 8. Security

All remote work must comply with the Information Security Policy. Key requirements include:
- Use the company VPN for all work activities
- Do not use public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Lock your screen when stepping away
- Do not allow non-employees to view or access company information
- Store company data only on approved devices and cloud services

## 9. Tax and Legal Considerations

Employees who work remotely from a state or country different from their office location must notify HR, as this may affect tax withholding, benefits, and labor law compliance. Working from abroad requires prior written approval from HR and Legal and is limited to 30 cumulative days per year.
