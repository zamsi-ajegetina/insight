# Business Travel Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Policy Owner:** Finance Department
**Applies To:** All employees of Meridian Technologies, Inc.

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for business travel at Meridian Technologies, ensuring that travel is conducted efficiently, safely, and in a cost-effective manner.

## 2. Travel Authorization

### 2.1 Pre-Trip Approval

All business travel must be pre-approved before booking:

| Trip Cost (estimated) | Approval Required |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| Under $1,000 | Direct manager |
| $1,000–$5,000 | Director-level |
| Over $5,000 | VP-level |
| International travel | VP-level + Legal notification |

### 2.2 Booking Process

All travel must be booked through the company's approved travel platform (currently **Navan**). Direct bookings with airlines or hotels are not reimbursable unless the booking platform is unavailable and manager approval is obtained.

Travel should be booked at least **14 days** in advance when possible to secure better rates. Last-minute bookings (under 7 days) require a brief written justification.

## 3. Air Travel

### 3.1 Class of Service

| Flight Duration | Approved Class |
|----------------|---------------|
| Under 4 hours | Economy |
| 4–6 hours | Economy (economy plus/comfort+ permitted) |
| Over 6 hours | Premium economy |
| Over 10 hours | Business class (with VP approval) |

First-class travel is not reimbursable without CFO approval.

### 3.2 Airline Selection

Employees should select the lowest reasonable fare available within 2 hours of the requested departure time. Loyalty program preferences are acceptable if the fare difference is **less than $100** compared to the lowest option.

### 3.3 Baggage

One checked bag is reimbursable for trips lasting 3+ days. Excess baggage requires justification.

## 4. Ground Transportation

### 4.1 Rental Cars

- Book through Navan using the company's negotiated rates
- Standard/mid-size vehicles unless group size requires larger
- Decline rental company insurance (covered by corporate policy)
- Refuel before returning the vehicle — refueling charges are not reimbursable
- Rental cars are authorized for destinations without adequate public transit or when more cost-effective than ride-sharing

### 4.2 Ride-Sharing and Taxis

- Standard service level (UberX, Lyft standard)
- Luxury services (Uber Black, Lyft Lux) are not reimbursable without client-facing justification
- Airport transfers via ride-share are preferred over airport parking for trips under 5 days

### 4.3 Personal Vehicle

- Mileage reimbursed at IRS standard rate ($0.67/mile for 2025)
- Tolls and parking reimbursed with receipts
- Personal vehicle use for trips over 200 miles one-way requires comparison against airfare — the lesser amount is reimbursed

## 5. Lodging

### 5.1 Hotel Selection

Use the Navan platform to book hotels with negotiated corporate rates. When corporate rates are unavailable:

| City Category | Maximum Nightly Rate |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Tier 1 (NYC, SF, DC, LA, Chicago, Boston, Seattle) | $250 |
| Tier 2 (other major metros: Denver, Austin, Miami, etc.) | $175 |
| Tier 3 (all other locations) | $125 |

Rates include taxes but exclude resort fees (which are reimbursable).

### 5.2 Extended Stays

For trips exceeding **5 nights**, employees should explore extended-stay hotels or serviced apartments, which typically offer lower rates. Airbnb is permitted for stays of 5+ nights with manager approval.

### 5.3 Hotel Policies

- Book cancellable rates when possible
- Room service is covered up to the per-meal limits in the Expense Policy
- Laundry service is reimbursable for trips exceeding 4 nights
- Minibar, in-room movies, and gym day passes are not reimbursable

## 6. Meals During Travel

### 6.1 Per Diem Option

Employees may opt for a flat per diem instead of itemized meal receipts:

| Location | Daily Per Diem |
|----------|---------------|
| Tier 1 cities | $75 |
| Tier 2 cities | $60 |
| All other | $50 |

Per diem covers all meals including tips. When using per diem, no meal receipts are required.

### 6.2 Itemized Option

Alternatively, employees may submit itemized receipts. Meal limits from the Expense Policy apply.

### 6.3 Meals Provided

When meals are provided (e.g., conference meals, client-provided meals), the per diem is reduced by $15 for breakfast, $20 for lunch, or $30 for dinner.

## 7. International Travel

### 7.1 Additional Requirements

International travelers must:
- Notify Legal at least **3 weeks** before departure
- Ensure passport validity of 6+ months beyond travel dates
- Obtain necessary visas (HR assists with business visa applications)
- Register the trip with the company's travel risk management service
- Download the emergency assistance app provided by IT

### 7.2 International Expenses

- Currency exchange fees are reimbursable
- International health insurance supplement is provided automatically
- Vaccinations and travel health consultations are reimbursable
- International SIM cards or roaming plans: up to $15/day reimbursable

## 8. Travel Safety

### 8.1 Travel Risk Levels

The company uses a 4-tier travel risk assessment:
- **Level 1 (Low)**: Normal travel, no restrictions
- **Level 2 (Moderate)**: Travel permitted with awareness
- **Level 3 (High)**: Travel requires VP + Security approval
- **Level 4 (Extreme)**: Travel prohibited

### 8.2 Emergency Assistance

24/7 travel emergency support is available at:
- Phone: +1-800-555-TRIP (8747)
- Email: travel-emergency@meridiantech.com
- App: Meridian Travel Assist (available on iOS and Android)

## 9. Travel Time

### 9.1 Non-Exempt Employees

All travel time during normal work hours is compensable. Travel time outside normal work hours is compensable only if the employee is actively working.

### 9.2 Exempt Employees

Exempt employees are not compensated additionally for travel time but may flex their schedule the following week with manager approval if travel occurs on weekends.
